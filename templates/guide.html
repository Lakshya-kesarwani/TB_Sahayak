Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs. Caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, it spreads through airborne droplets and remains one of the top infectious disease killers worldwide.

Transmission:
  • Airborne spread through coughs/sneezes
  • Close, prolonged contact required
  • Not spread through surfaces or touch
Prevention:
  • BCG vaccination in high-risk areas
  • Proper ventilation in living spaces
  • Early detection and treatment

Common Symptoms:
  • Persistent cough (3+ weeks)
  • Chest pain and breathlessness
  • Unintentional weight loss
  • Night sweats and fever
Diagnostic Methods:
  • Skin test (TST)
  • Blood tests (IGRA)
  • Chest X-ray
  • Sputum culture

  • Requires specialized regimens
  • 18-24 month treatment duration
  • Newer drugs like Bedaquiline
